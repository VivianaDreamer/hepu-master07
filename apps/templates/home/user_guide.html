¡Welcome to HEPU!

HEPU is a web-based software-as-a-service tool to design and perform feasibility studies of hydrogen projects. It is oriented to project developers and project financiers, as it increases quality and auditability of hydrogen development portfolios, de-risking the decision making process for the stake-holder

Technic design

Your design will be divided in two steps, the technical specifications is the first one and treats about the description of your project without talking about their costs yet, it means, submit parameters related with your production and answering questions about when and how. It’s divided into three parts:

General

  • Project name: You can put the name that you want but we suggest a functional one because this will be the name of the downloaded file.
  • Location: Write the city or the country of your project, this is just for reference and doesn’t have any implication in your results.
  • Start of production: This is the year that your electrolyzer will start to degrade and will be used to calculate the year of change stack.
  • Years of operation: Horizon of your project, is important for the technical and economics results.

Energy supply

Energy input: Select between Power purchase agreement (PPA) or your own generation vector.

  • PPA option allows you to choose between a 24/7 generation or a customized one, where you must select the start and the finish hour of your production. For both you must enter the PPA nominal power in MegaWatts (MW).
  • User defined generation vector option allows you to upload a file in 4 formats (TMY, TMY_SAM, TMY_PVsol and PVSyst) and the nominal power too.

Electrolysis

Includes all the electrochemical and physical parameters of the electrolyzer. If you don't know the respective data, please keep the current ones.

  • Electrolyzer type: Select between Polymer electrolyte membrane (PEM) and Alkaline (ALK) electrolysis. A Specific energy consumption versus Power input graph will be available for your analysis. Solid oxide electrolyzer cell (SOEC) will be available soon.
  • Electrolyzer nominal power: Must be indicated by the manufacturing company and entered in MegaWatts (MW).
  • Min. electrolyzer efficiency before replacement [%]: Your electrolyzer will be replaced whe the effiency decrease to this point.
  • Water supply: Select between “Tap water”or “Demi water”, it depends on your own disponibility.
  • PEM electrolyzer
    • Cell area [cm²]: Active area responsible for the conduction of protons, separation of product gases, and electrical insulation of the electrodes
    • Thermoneutral voltage [V]: Voltage drop across the electrochemical cell
    • Transfer coefficient alpha: Kinetic parameter employed in activation and concentration overpotential
    • Limiting current density [A/cm²]: Maximum current density required to achieve the desired electrode reaction.
    • Membrane thickness [cm]: Increase factor on the ohmic protonic overpotential
    • Membrane hydration parameter: Decrease factor on the ohmic protonic overpotential
    • Roughness factor anode [cm²/cm²]: Physical property for anode involved in activation overpotential.
    • Roughness factor cathode[cm²/cm²]: Physical property for cathode involved in activation overpotential.
    • Anode exchange current density at the ref. T: Electrochemical property for anode involved in activation overpotential.
    • Cathode exchange current density at the ref. T: Electrochemical property for cathode involved in activation overpotential.
    • Reference Temperature [K]: Standar condition temperature
    • Nominal operating temperature [K]: Electrolyzer working temperature
    • Anode activation energy [J/mol]: Energy lost due to the slowness of electrochemical reactions
    • Cathode activation energy [J/mol]: Energy lost due to the slowness of electrochemical reactions

Costs and financial inputs design

This is the second and last step of your design, you might not know all the parameters requested but try to get the best approximation to them for a good analysis, some of them are suggested for a better comprehension but remind that it can change for every different project.

CAPEX

  • Specific Electrolyzer and BoP cost [USD/kW]: The sum of the specific cost of your electrolyzer and the balance of plant, i.e. the necessary components for the hydrogen production.
  • Specific Development cost [USD/kW]: Include the costs of the researching, growing and introducing your new project.
  • Installation cost [USD/kW]: Total cost of labor and materials for your start of production.
  • Indirect costs [USD/project]: Any CAPEX cost not included before
  • Total CAPEX [USD/kW]: This section shows the sum of your three previous inputs.

OPEX

  • Energy cost [USD/MWh]: Cost of the electrical supply, it might come from a renewable or a fossil font.
  • O&M [USD/kW/year]: Operation and maintenance costs. If you don’t know this number you can suppose a 20% of the sum of the Specific Electrolyzer and BoP cost and Specific Development cost.
  • Water cost [USD/m3]: It depends if you will work with tap or demi water, and where do you get your water from. Countries can have different regulations for this item.
  • Land cost [USD/ha/year]: Can be very variable but consider the magnitude of your project, the land of the plant and the corresponding administration.

Finance

  • Equity discount rate [%]:interest rate used in discounted cash flow analysis to determine the present value of future cash flows.
  • Debt [%]: Percentage of capital that is debt.
  • Debt term [years]: Horizon of your debt.
  • Interest Rate [%]: Cost in percentage of your borrowing.
  • First category Tax [%]: Basic tax on income of a legal entity domiciled or resident in Chile and engaged in commerce, mining, fishing, or industrial activities

Simulation

Once you put all the data in their place, you must select the “Simulation” button, the software will advise you if there is something missing.

The software will take all your data and will start running, this can take a few seconds.

Firstly, a yearly energy input vector will be created depending on your energy supply data. Then, according to your electrolysis definition, consumption will be calculated by taking cell parameters and transforming them into values on the polarization curve. Degradation depends on your electrolyzer type, it will be used to obtain the year of stack replacements and calculate the hydrogen production decrease. Oxygen production and water consumption depends on this too.

Secondly, when all the technical data is processed, is cost’s turn. CAPEX is the sum of the CAPEX inputs section and OPEX depends on hours of operation and type of degradation than, including water costs, allows to get the annual operational and replacements costs.

Results

Performance summary year 1

  • Hydrogen production year 1 [tons/year]: Divided by months and considering their different quantity of days.
  • Oxygen production year 1 [tons/year]: It corresponds to 8 times the hydrogen production.
  • Energy consumption year 1 [GWh/year]: Depends on the energy input and the size of your project.
  • Electrolyzer load factor [%]: Average load over peak load.
  • Average sp. energy consumption [kWh/kgH2]: Energy consumption per Hydrogen kilogram.
  • Water consumption year 1 [m3/year]: Variates if you select demi or tap water and, of course, your production of hydrogen.
  • Energy curtailed [GWh/year]: Quantity of energy that it’s not being used for hydrogen production.
  • Energy cost [USD/year]: Total energy cost for year 1.
  • Water cost [USD/year]: Total water cost for year 1.
  • Production per month graph: Year 1 hydrogen production detailed monthly.

Costs during life cycle

  • Total CAPEX [USD]: Sum of the electrolyzer and BoP, development and land cost considering the size of the plant.
  • Present Cost of replacements [USD]: Considering the total of these.
  • Stack replacements: Number of stack replacements you must do in the horizon of your project.
  • Year of replacements: Quantity of years after your start of production when you must make the replacement.
  • WACC: Weighted Average Cost of Capital.
  • LCOH [USD/kgH2]: Levelized Cost of Hydrogen. Calculated by the sum of your CAPEX, energy, O&M, water and stack costs over the outputs.
  • Hydrogen production during project life (graphic): Horizon’s hydrogen production detailed annually.
  • Energy performance during life cycle (graphic): Horizon’s energy consumption per kilogram of hydrogen detailed annually.
